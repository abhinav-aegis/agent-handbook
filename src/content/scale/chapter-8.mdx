## Observability, Runtime Assurance, and Feedback Loops

- Why static evaluation is not enough once systems act at runtime
- What needs to be observable: context used, decisions made, tools invoked, actions taken
- Runtime assurance: guardrails, policy enforcement, validation, human override
- Feedback loops from users, workflows, and outcomes back into improvement
- The relationship between tracing, monitoring, assurance, and trust

**Why we are writing the chapter**

To explain how safe systems stay safe after deployment. This chapter focuses on the operating discipline customers need once AI is embedded in real work. It also maps to high-value capabilities customers care about: visibility, assurance, override, and continuous improvement.
