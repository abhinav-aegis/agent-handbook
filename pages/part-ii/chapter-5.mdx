## Identity, Authority, and Policy-Bound Action

- The difference between decision-making and permission to act
- Why “acting on behalf of” needs explicit identity and delegation
- How user identity, agent identity, and system identity interact
- Tool access, authorization, and policy checks before execution
- Why least privilege and scoped authority matter for agent systems

**Why we are writing the chapter**

To address tool misuse and action without clear authority. This chapter makes the core design principle concrete: separate what the system can know from what it is allowed to do. It also maps to a major customer value area: governed action, explicit delegation, and safer automation.
